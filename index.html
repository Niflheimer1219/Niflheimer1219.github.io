FOREWARD

  • Nihilo is a survival-horror exploration game. Within, the player is tasked to navigate through a series of dungeons wherein there are many philosophical and violent conundrums. Though the concept of consistently invoking philosophical issues is not uncommon, and is often cumbersome for those that encounter them, Nihilo is intended to, essentially, provide a gameplay loop which will act as a vehicle for the player to encounter each conundrum with frequency determined by the player themselves.

    The player will encounter many surreal, horrific events showing the consequences of other’s actions, or worse, their own actions. While the player is not able to save these individuals, it will likely, hopefully, result in the player feeling un-encumbered. Alternatively, those seeking the thrill of death alone may wantonly choose their own fate in order to greatly ramp up the difficulty of the game, and the scenarios they’re placed in.

    The player character will be consistently pursued by a Nightmare Effigy however the player can evade these Nightmare Effigies from time to time, allowing time for reprieve. By approaching their predicament with creativity, using tools and items found throughout the gameworld, the player may be able to delay or stop their pursuer. Player actions are recorded when making Critical Decisions; cataloguing player actions, and adding or removing points that affect how the game later represents itself to the player. This is a critical, underpinning element to the game, known historically as Psychological Profiling.

    CHAPTERS

  • Chapter One: The Font

  • Regardless of who the player picks, the protagonist begins their journey at a stone font raised non-descriptly somewhere in the Vast Expanse.

    As they begin interacting with this font, a sphere emerges from the recess of the font. Upon touching this sphere, three large pyramids begin rising from the still sands, pushing and toppling ancient corpses trapped beneath the land. The three pyramids are located directly north, east, and west of the font. The north pyramid begins emitting a low hum that raises into a booming, hungering groan. Cyclically, every few moments, the hum raises into a boom, then recedes, only to raise again. The player, upon viewing an image of each pyramid, spots an entrance to the western pyramid, where upon drawing near, a violent sand storm begins to pick up. Approaching the entrance, the sand blows harder and harder, turning slowly into longer glass shards. Should the player not enter the relative safety of this mysterious structure, they’d surely be violently torn into ribbons by the now storming flurry of glass.
    Some time passes in the musty, congested dark alone before the storm clears. Once clear, the player looks out the opening, observing the landscape once more: Scarred–no, pock-marked–earth stretches seemingly beyond even the horizon. It is hideous and barren. Holes can be seen before, aside, and beyond the font the player sees out in the now distant stretch recently sprinted across. How deep they go, it must be endless as far as can be discerned now. Even the “fresh” air coming in is accosting; strangulating hot and fetid smelling. Limbs, whole bodies, and faces jut lifelessly from the rusted, dark-brown dunes. the protagonist turns back ‘round to see a hallway just beyond sight, in the shadows. It stretches into the dark, but not without obvious omen. A slipping, smacking sound comes from deep within the dark.

    Smack, smack, smack…

    A muffled, desperate howl echoes from the dark as the noises get louder, and a light appears within the dark. What appears to be a man is frantically rushing down the hall, and now towards the protagonist. He is flayed, mouth clearly covered shut by a sheet of iron barbarically hammered into the surrounding skull. He flails and screams manically, so desperately, to escape the agony, but ultimately collapses at his feet before the player, a large pool of blood then accumulating. The wretch appears to be male, adult, and absolutely without skin. In addition to this, he has been very clearly harmed before that–stab wounds and clear separations of muscle pronounce this. A torch, apparently, has been crudely bound to the poor man’s left hand. Without this light source, the protagonist cannot see down the hall. However, the player is not restricted to only taking this torch: they may enter without a light source. hould the player decide to take the torch, they will be forced to twist the dead man’s hand off in order to retrieve the torch. The torch comes off with an audible, sickening crack. The player raises the torch, remnants of the man’s hands still bound to the middle of the haft. The dark stretches into the hallway, now illuminated somewhat. With few other options, the protagonist ventures forward.
